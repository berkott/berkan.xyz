I wanna get better at thinking and doing research and here’s how I’m going to try to do so.

  • Pay attention to the strength of arguments and the argument structure. Are they soely deductive claims? Do they withstand argumentation? Are assumptions justified? Reminder, Deduction, induction, abduction. Deduction is just pushing facts, induction is generalizing, and abduction is expanding the hypothesis space. Claude gave a good example:
    • Deduction. Rule: all beans from this bag are white. Case: these beans are from this bag. ∴ Result: these beans are white.
    • Induction. Case: these beans are from this bag. Result: these beans are white. ∴ Rule: all beans from this bag are white.
    • Abduction. Rule: all beans from this bag are white. Result: these beans are white. ∴ Case: these beans are from this bag.
  • Consider framing research as an RL problem with sparse reward on some data.Obviously I want to create a rich feedback signal on high quality data. In research this means getting lots of feedback from myself and others, and consuming content that I can build good abstractions from. More specifically I can
    • Look at a paper problem statement and abstract and predict what their central thread and main results are
    • Cleanly break papers down into individual argument steps and reconstruct the paper from just those steps
    • Ask for feedback on my ideas and experiments often and before everything is finished.
    • Make connections between papers
  • Goal directed research a la John Schulman
  • Take ownership and turn they into we. I thought they were thinking about xyz problem… No, it’s we!
